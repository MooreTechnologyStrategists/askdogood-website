From Sick Patient to Healing Writer

Writing saved my life before I even knew I was dying. At 12 years old, I started journaling my symptoms when doctors couldn't figure out what was wrong with me. Those early notebooks became my first health detective work.

Through high school, I wrote poetry about feeling different, about energy crashes, about watching friends eat foods that made me sick. I didn't know I was documenting autoimmune disease - I just knew writing helped me process the isolation.

When I finally got my Graves disease diagnosis at 22, I started blogging my healing journey. Every supplement tried, every food eliminated, every small victory documented. Those raw, honest posts connected with people going through similar struggles.

Now my writing serves a bigger purpose: turning my pain into protocols that actually help people heal. Every article comes from lived experience, every tip has been tested in my own body first.

I write because when you're sick and searching for answers at 3 AM, you need someone who's been there to tell you what really works.
